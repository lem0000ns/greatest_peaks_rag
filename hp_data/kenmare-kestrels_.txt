Kenmare Kestrels 
"Seamus did not answer immediately; he was making rather a meal of ensuring that his poster of the Kenmare Kestrels Quidditch team was quite straight. Then he said, with his back still turned to Harry, 'Me mam didn't want me to come back.'" (OP11)
The Kenmare Kestrels are a professional Quidditch team based in Kenmare, County Kerry, Republic of Ireland. They were founded in 1291 (QA7).
robes: emerald-green with two yellow Ks back to back on the front (QA7)
home: Kenmare
mascots: leprechauns (like those of Ireland’s National Team) (QA7).
Darren O’Hare, Keeper (1947-60) – he invented the Hawkshead Attacking Formation (QA7).
Aidan Kiely, Seeker (1990s) (DP2)
The team’s supporters play harps (QA7).
Seamus Finnegan is a fan (OP11).
Donaghan Tremlett (1972-present), a bass player with The Weird Sisters, is also a “big fan” of the Kestrels (FW).
Kenmare is a town in County Kerry, Ireland. There are a few sports teams listed in its Wikipedia entry (Gaelic football, football and basketball), but no Quidditch teams (Wikipedia).
